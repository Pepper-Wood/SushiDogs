Sushi Dogs, also called Sooshes, are tiny canine creatures that resemble foods and drinks from around the globe. Sushi Dogs are named as such because they are roughly the size of a single piece of sushi. They are iconically known for having closed eyes, colorful cheeks, and swirl bandanas. The Sushi Dog online community consists of collectors and artists who enjoy making artwork of and trading Sushi Dog characters; only authorized moderators can create official Sushi Dogs, which adds to their rarity.

In April 2017, I became a moderator of the deviantArt groups and extended my web development skills through projects for two different facets of the community:

  • Sushidogs - The original deviantArt group that accepts artwork of Sushi Dogs
  • SooshARPG - The Art Role-Playing Game counterpart to Sushidogs. Members in this deviantArt group can participate in a wide assortment of activities where they can gather items and boost their character's stats through art prompts.

Sushi Dogs were created by Jess / SpikedPeach / blushsprout and are co-lead by Bryanna "Noxi" Davidson / UFOKitten.

SooshARPG 2.0

Last Updated: 1/8/2017

While working on modifying the moderator tools and the Kumo tracker repositories, I made a new repository dedicated to recreating the tools from scratch. The key hightlights so far:

  • There is 1 master spreadsheet that can be editted by the mods for keeping track of important statistics.
  • This spreadsheet contains links to individual members' spreadsheets for their own trackers.
  • Each character's page starts with a standard template that is populated from either just the moderator spreadsheet or both the mod + member spreadsheets
  • Moderator tools linked are now password protected with pseudo-security: I've hashed the correct password and hard-coded this into the JS, along with the hashing function (due to GitHub's lack of server-side restrictions).

SooshARPG Moderator Tools

Last Updated: 5/8/2017

When I became a moderator for SooshARPG in April 2017, one of my first tasks assigned to me was to perform "dailies". This meant: if a member requested their soosh to perform a skill (like hunting, fishing, etc.), then a moderator comments whether the soosh was successful and gives items/experience points. This task was really tedious to do by hand, so I immediately went to make a roller myself. This repo has since expanded into other moderator tools. This project is on hiatus as I have continued my work in SooshARPG 2.0.
Soosh Database Webpage

Last Updated: 4/30/2017

In the early days as a moderator, I wanted to make an easy-to-update database for all the sushi dogs. There is currently a deviantArt account that maintains these records (sooshDatabase on deviantArt), but I wanted to add an easy way to keep track of how a soosh was transferred, i.e. through trade, through sale, etc. The page pulls from a google spreadsheet with multiple tabs dedicated to different portions of information. I am no longer updating the spreadsheet but am proud of the result.
